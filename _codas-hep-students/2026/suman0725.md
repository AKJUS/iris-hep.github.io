## My research:

I am a Ph.D. student in Physics at Temple University working with the CLAS12 Collaboration at Jefferson Lab. My research focuses on semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) to study nuclear effects and transverse momentum-dependent (TMD) distributions using pion production from nuclear targets.

## My expertise is:

Experimental nuclear physics, SIDIS data analysis, Monte Carlo simulations, ROOT, and scientific computing with the CLAS12 experiment.

## A problem I'm grappling with:

Developing robust and reproducible analysis workflows that accurately account for detector acceptance, radiative corrections, and systematic uncertainties in SIDIS measurements.

## I've got my eyes on:

Improving precision measurements of nuclear structure with CLAS12 while developing modern, reproducible software tools for large-scale physics analyses.

## I want to know more about:

Research software engineering, high-performance computing, collaborative open-source software development, workflow automation, and modern data analysis techniques for particle and nuclear physics.
